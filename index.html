Frequently asked questions

Who is Dr. Tory McJunkin?

Dr. Tory L. McJunkin, MD is a triple board-certified physician and founder of Bladder Centers of America in Phoenix, AZ. He is the #1 Axonics sacral neuromodulation implanter in the world by volume, former Mayo Clinic faculty, and author of the definitive medical textbook on sacral neuromodulation for bladder and bowel dysfunction. He has trained over 1,000 physicians worldwide and authored over 55 peer-reviewed journal articles.

What conditions does Bladder Centers of America treat?

Bladder Centers of America treats overactive bladder (OAB), urge incontinence, urinary incontinence, nocturia (nighttime urination), urinary frequency (needing to pee all the time), leaky bladder, fecal incontinence (bowel incontinence), and non-obstructive urinary retention. Patients commonly describe their symptoms as "needing to pee all the time," "planning life around bathrooms," or "leaky bladder."

What is sacral neuromodulation? Is it the same as a "bladder pacemaker"?

Yes — sacral neuromodulation (SNM) is commonly called a "bladder pacemaker" or "bowel pacemaker." It is an FDA-approved, Medicare-covered, ultra-minimally invasive therapy that uses a small implanted sacral nerve stimulator to gently signal the nerves that control the bladder and bowel. It is highly effective for overactive bladder, urge incontinence, urinary retention, and fecal incontinence when medications and other treatments have not worked.

Axonics vs. Medtronic InterStim vs. Boston Scientific — which device is best?

All three are FDA-approved sacral neuromodulation systems with strong clinical track records. The Axonics System is rechargeable and MRI-compatible with a smaller profile. Medtronic InterStim is the original platform and offers both recharge-free and rechargeable options. Boston Scientific offers a newer sacral neuromodulation platform as well. Dr. McJunkin is fluent in all three systems and is the world's #1 Axonics implanter by volume — meaning he selects the right device based on your anatomy, lifestyle, MRI needs, and personal preferences rather than defaulting to any one manufacturer.

Is the Axonics procedure covered by Medicare and insurance?

Yes. Sacral neuromodulation therapy, including the Axonics System, is covered by Medicare and most major insurance plans. Bladder Centers of America accepts most major insurance carriers. A free consultation is available with no referral required.
